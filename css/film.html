Film 59

Psammophylax rhombeatus, rubbing and self-rubbing

What is causing the remarkable rubbing behavior of these two skaapstekers?

In the first fragment, we see one of the animals slide along the other one, and then suddenly start rubbing the lateral side of its cagemate's body. (Both animals are so much alike that it is very hard to distinguish who if who during the action.)
In the next section (a couple of minutes after the first scene), we see them in what seems to be an introduction to mating. (By the way: I still don't know the sex of those animals, though there has been a lot of behavior that looks like rivalry fight between males. They have done this mainly in the evening, when the lights are down, but also once and a while by day. And again, on the other hand, I saw behavior that looked like mating behavior.)
In the third section, one of them is again rubbing the other, rather irregularly. Then the animals turns around and starts a self-rubbing session. Around the 60th head movement, it stops, when the other snake starts to grab him in the back a couple of times, and then starts to rub him too.
